Relational Discernment

We don't just evaluate ministries. We spend time with the people who lead them.

Documents reveal structure. Conversations reveal intention. Proximity reveals character. The most important things about a leader cannot be captured in a form or an interview. For select organizations, our team spends real time in proximity with ministry leadership — observing the consistency between what is presented and what is lived.

This step is mutual and invitational. It is never entered without the ministry's full knowledge and consent. It is reserved for organizations being considered for significant giving relationships — a mark of trust, not suspicion.

Integrity is not proven in presentation. It is confirmed in consistency — over time, in proximity, in the ordinary.

What this includes

  • Time spent in person with ministry leadership
  • Shared meals and unstructured conversation
  • Observation of leadership rhythms and team culture
  • Relational interaction outside formal or prepared settings
  • Conversations with those closest to the leader
Why it matters

Discernment is not doubt.
It is wisdom.

Billions of dollars flow to Christian ministries every year. Very little of it is vetted. Most donors give based on relationship, reputation, or a compelling story.

Those are not bad starting points. But they are not enough.

Asking hard questions is an act of love. The organizations you fund represent your values. Vetting strengthens the bond between donor and mission.
Accountability protects the mission. Healthy ministries welcome scrutiny. The vetting process itself is a spiritual discipline.
Fruit requires roots. We look at governance, doctrine, and character — not just outputs — because unsustainable ministries waste generosity.

The model

A 100-point framework built on what actually matters.

Six weighted categories. Explicit subcriteria. Automatic flag triggers. Hard stop disqualifiers. Nothing arbitrary.

20 pts

Leadership Integrity

Accountability structure, board independence, decision model, personal character and stability.

15 pts

Doctrine

Gospel clarity, Scripture position, public doctrinal statement, staff affirmation requirements.

15 pts

Governance

Board independence, meeting frequency, conflict-of-interest policy, whistleblower protections.

20 pts

Financial Stewardship

Audit level, program expense ratio, reserve fund, restricted fund compliance and transparency.

20 pts

Fruit

Scale of impact, theory of change, spiritual measurement method, third-party validation.

10 pts

External Trust

ECFA membership, reference quality, IRS record, news and reputation review.

85–100 Strongly recommended
70–84 Recommended
55–69 Conditional
Below 55 Not recommended
